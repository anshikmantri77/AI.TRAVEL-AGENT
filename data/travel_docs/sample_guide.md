# Paris, France — Local Knowledge Guide

## Hidden Gems (Beyond the Guidebooks)

1. **Passage des Panoramas** — One of the oldest covered arcades in Paris
   (1799).  Tucked behind the Grands Boulevards, it is lined with
   stamp dealers, vintage print shops, and excellent bistros that
   tourists rarely find.  Free to enter.
2. **Promenade Plantée** — An elevated park built on a disused railway
   viaduct stretching 4.7 km from Bastille to the Bois de Vincennes.
   Quieter than the more famous Coulée verte sections and offers
   unexpected views of the city's rooftop landscape.
3. **Musée Jacquemart-André** — A 19th-century mansion turned museum
   with a stunning private art collection (Rembrandt, Botticelli,
   Tiepolo) and a tearoom in the original grand salon.  Far less
   crowded than the Louvre or Orsay.
4. **Île de la Cité — Square du Vert-Galant** — A tiny triangular park
   at the western tip of the island.  Perfect sunset picnic spot
   watched mainly by locals; the Pont Neuf looms overhead.
5. **La Butte aux Cailles** — A village-like neighbourhood in the 13th
   arrondissement with cobblestone streets, affordable bistros, and
   street-art covered facades.  A genuine alternative to Montmartre
   without the crowds.

## Local Food Tips

- **Avoid restaurants with photo menus** in multiple languages near
  major landmarks.  Walk two streets away for better food and half the
  price.
- **French lunch formula** (entrée + plat + dessert) is usually
  €15–22 and almost always better value than ordering à la carte.
  Look for "formule" or "menu du jour" on chalkboards.
- **Fromagerie etiquette**: let the cheesemonger guide your selection.
  Say "Je voudrais un plateau pour deux personnes" and specify
  "affiné" (aged) if you prefer stronger flavours.

## Transport Hacks

- **Navigo Découverte pass**: For €30/week it covers unlimited travel
  in zones 1–5 (including both airports).  Requires a passport photo
  and is available at any metro station booth.  Much cheaper than
  single tickets.
- **Vélib' e-bikes**: The city's bike-sharing scheme has electric
  bikes at €5 for a day pass.  The first 30 minutes of each ride are
  free with the pass.  Stations are everywhere — look for the grey
  terminals.

## Safety Note

Paris is generally safe, but be vigilant in crowded areas (Métro
lines 1, 4, and 13; around Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur).  Keep bags
zipped and avoid displaying valuables on the RER B line to/from
Charles de Gaulle airport.  The 10th and 18th arrondissements have
pockets of petty theft, especially around Gare du Nord and Gare de
l'Est after dark.
